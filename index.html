What is a refugee?

A refugee is any person who has been forced to leave their home. This could be due to War, violence, oppressive government, persecution, or natural disaster. Most countries in the UN accept refugees into their borders and provide humanitarian aid. However, often refugee camps are set up in neighboring countries to provide convenient aid.

n the Abbasid empire, Christians and Jews had to pay a tax to ensure their protection. Those of neither religion, or those not wanting to pay the tax, were often displaced and forced to move by the government.

1300 A.D. - 1800 A.D.

The Slave Trade was one of the largest forced migration of people in history. 12.7 million refugees were shipped from Africa to the US via the triangle trade.

1800 A.D. 2000 A.D.

After WWII, during decolonization, France left Algeria in a rush, not giving the proper time and resources to ensure stability. Fighting broke out, and civil strife is just being resolved now. Multiple Sudanese Civil Wars took place during this time period and 4 million were displaced

2000s A.D.

Syrian Refugees are currently fleeing the civil war in Syria, and have fled mainly to the EU via the mediterranean sea (and of that, mainly Germany)
